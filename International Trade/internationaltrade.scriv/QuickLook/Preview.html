      The Japanese economy is an industrialized, free-market economy. It ranks fourth in the world in GDP -  after the U.S., China, and India - in terms of international purchasing power parity. With cooperation between government and industry, a strong work ethic, mastery of industrial technology, and a small defense outlay (1% of GDP compared to 4.7% of GDP of the US defense expenditure budget), Japan has managed to become the second most technologically-powerful economy in the world.
